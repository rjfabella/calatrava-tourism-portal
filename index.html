About Calatrava

A hidden gem on the northern tip of Tablas Island

Nestled on the northernmost coast of Tablas — the largest island in Romblon — Calatrava is a municipality of unspoiled beaches, dramatic waterfalls, and warm island hospitality. Budget-friendly and uncrowded, it offers what few destinations still can: genuine island life.

Things to do in Calatrava

01
Island Hopping

Explore limestone caves, hidden coves, and crystal-clear waters around Guindawahan Island and Tinagong Dagat. Book through our accredited operators at the Calatrava Port Terminal.

From ₱3,000 for 10 persons
02
Waterfall Trekking

Six waterfalls across four barangays — from the dramatic Tago Falls, the highest in Calatrava at 7 to 9 meters, to the secluded Hidden Falls deep in Pagsangahan.

Guide fees apply
03
Snorkeling & Diving

LGU-accredited dive groups offer guided snorkeling and diving around vibrant reef ecosystems and rich marine life in the municipality's protected coastal waters.

Rates vary by operator
