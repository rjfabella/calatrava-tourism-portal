About Calatrava

A hidden gem on the northern tip of Tablas Island

Nestled on the northernmost coast of Tablas — the largest island in Romblon — Calatrava is a municipality of unspoiled beaches, dramatic waterfalls, and warm island hospitality. Budget-friendly and uncrowded, it offers what few destinations still can: genuine island life.

Things to do in Calatrava

Photo: RJ Fabella
01 / Island Hopping
Chart Unmapped Coves

Sail between dramatic karst formations and anchor in hidden pockets of white sand where your footprints may be the only ones there. Book through our accredited operators at the Calatrava Port Terminal.

From ₱3,000 for 10 persons
Photo: Calatrava Tourism Council
02 / Waterfall Trekking
Chase the Hidden Falls

Trade the beach for the hills. Jungle trails lead to six waterfalls across four barangays — from the dramatic Tago Falls, the highest in Calatrava, to the secluded Hidden Falls deep in Pagsangahan.

Guide fees apply
Photo: Sisira PH
03 / Snorkeling & Diving
Peer Into the Deep Blue

Calatrava's marine sanctuaries thrive just off the shoreline. Put on a mask with an LGU-accredited dive group and discover vibrant coral gardens teeming with life in protected coastal waters.

Rates vary by operator
